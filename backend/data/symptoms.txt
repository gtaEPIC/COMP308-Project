## Symptom List and When to See a Doctor

**Symptoms**

* Fever: A body temperature above 100.4째F (38째C) for adults or 102째F (38.9째C) for children.

* Severe chest pain: Sharp, crushing pain that lasts for more than a few minutes.

* Abdominal pain: Persistent, severe pain, especially if accompanied by nausea, vomiting, or bloody stools.

* Shortness of breath: Difficulty breathing, even at rest.

* Coughing up blood: Blood in your mucus when you cough.

* Severe headache: A headache that is unusually severe or accompanied by other symptoms like nausea, vomiting, or confusion.

* Confusion: Disorientation, difficulty thinking clearly, or slurred speech.

* Seizures: Uncontrolled shaking or convulsions.

* Loss of consciousness: Fainting or passing out.

* Severe or persistent vomiting or diarrhea: Dehydration can become a concern.

* Severe or persistent dizziness or lightheadedness: Could indicate an underlying medical condition.

* Numbness or tingling in extremities: May be a sign of nerve damage.

* Persistent skin rash: Could be a sign of an allergic reaction or other skin condition.

* Swelling in legs or ankles: May indicate heart or kidney problems.

* Persistent pain or swelling in joints: Could be a sign of arthritis or injury.

* Changes in vision: Blurred vision, floaters, or flashes of light.

* Changes in hearing: Ringing in the ears, hearing loss, or muffled hearing.

* Changes in speech or swallowing: Difficulty speaking or swallowing.

**When to See a Doctor**

* Any symptom that is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other concerning symptoms.

* Fever that lasts for more than 3 days in adults or 5 days in children.

* Severe chest pain, especially if accompanied by shortness of breath.

* Abdominal pain that is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other symptoms like nausea, vomiting, or bloody stools.

* Shortness of breath that is severe, persistent, or occurs with exertion.

* Coughing up blood.

* Severe headache, especially if accompanied by other symptoms like nausea, vomiting, or confusion.

* Confusion, seizures, or loss of consciousness.

* Severe or persistent vomiting or diarrhea.

* Severe or persistent dizziness or lightheadedness.

* Numbness or tingling in extremities.

* Persistent skin rash.

* Swelling in legs or ankles.

* Persistent pain or swelling in joints.

* Changes in vision or hearing.

* Changes in speech or swallowing.

* Any new or unusual symptoms.
